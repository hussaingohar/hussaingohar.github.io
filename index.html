About Me

I am an Assistant Professor at the Institute of Physics, University of Szczecin, Poland, specializing in theoretical physics with a focus on gravitation, cosmology, and the thermodynamic aspects of the universe.

My research explores the fundamental connections between gravity and thermodynamics, investigating phenomena such as dark energy models, entropic cosmology, nonextensive black hole thermodynamics, and the Hubble tension. I am particularly interested in developing unified frameworks that connect cosmological horizons with thermodynamic principles.

Research Philosophy

My approach combines rigorous mathematical formalism with physical intuition to address fundamental questions about the cosmos. I believe in the power of thermodynamic principles to unlock secrets of gravitational phenomena and am dedicated to exploring alternative approaches to cosmological problems.

Research Focus

Research Interests

My research explores fundamental questions at the intersection of gravity, thermodynamics, and cosmology

Dark Energy

Investigating the nature of dark energy through holographic principles and alternative models that can explain the accelerated expansion of the universe and address observational tensions.

Data Analysis

Applying statistical methods and computational techniques to analyze cosmological data, test theoretical models, and constrain parameters of alternative gravity theories.

read more

Scalar-Tensor Gravity Theories

Exploring modifications to general relativity through scalar-tensor theories and their implications for cosmological evolution and gravitational phenomena.

Gravity-Thermodynamic Correspondence

Studying the deep connections between gravitational field equations and thermodynamic laws, including entropic cosmology and nonextensive black hole thermodynamics.
